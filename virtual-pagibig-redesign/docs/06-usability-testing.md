# 06 — Usability Testing

## Test Plan

- **Participants:** 5 people (reuse research interviewees if possible, or new participants)
- **Format:** Share your Figma prototype in **Present** mode (screen share or in person), or export a shareable prototype link for remote testing
- **When:** Test wireframes first (catches structural problems cheaply); optionally retest with hi-fi mockups before final prototype polish

## Tasks to Give Participants

1. "Check your total savings and tell me if a contribution went through this month."
2. "Find out if you're eligible to apply for a housing loan."
3. "Something seems wrong with one of your contributions — try to report it."
4. "Go to your account settings and check your registered mobile number."

## What to Watch For

- Do they notice and trust the masked-MID reveal control?
- Do they understand the grouped loan sections (view vs. apply)?
- Is the new "Report a Posting Issue" flow discoverable without prompting?
- Do they hesitate or look confused at any screen transition?
- Where do they click first on each screen (compare to where you intended)?

## Synthesis

After all sessions, write 3–5 bullet findings, e.g.:
- 4/5 users found the labeled dashboard summary immediately reassuring — validates fixing heuristic issue #1
- 2/5 users didn't notice the masked-MID reveal icon on first glance — consider making it more visually distinct
- Document what you changed in the hi-fi screens as a result
